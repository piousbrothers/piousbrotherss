Morning

1. Wake Up for Fajr (Dawn Prayer)
Intention (Niyyah): Wake up with the intention to seek Allah's pleasure and start your day productively. Perform Wudu (ablution) to refresh and cleanse yourself. Pray Fajr Salah with focus and mindfulness. Reflect on the Quranic verses recited during the prayer.
-------------------------
2. Morning Supplications (Adhkar)
Spend a few minutes reciting morning adhkar (dhikr).

Say Dua for protection and blessings for the day ahead.

3. Hydrate and Energize
Drink a glass of water, ideally with dates or honey, as the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) recommended these for nourishment.
---------------
4. Personal Hygiene
Brush your teeth (using a miswak if possible). Take a refreshing shower, clean yourself, and dress in comfortable, modest clothing.
---------------
5. Read or Listen to the Quran
Spend 10–15 minutes reading or listening to the Quran. Reflect on its meaning and think about how to implement its teachings during the day.
---------------
6. Engage in Physical Activity
Do light stretches or a short workout to energize your body. Physical health is encouraged in Islam to fulfill your duties effectively.
---------------
7. Spread Positivity
Smile and greet others with Assalamu Alaikum, spreading blessings and peace. Start your day with kindness and a positive mindset.
----------------
8. Stay Grateful and Mindful Throughout the Day
Continuously renew your intention (niyyah) in all actions.

Noon



A Muslim’s noon routine often involves fulfilling both spiritual and worldly responsibilities.

1. Dhuhr Prayer (Obligatory)

Time: Performed after the sun has passed its zenith until mid-afternoon.

Perform wudu (ablution) if not already in a state of purity.

2. Offer 4 rakats Sunnah, followed by 4 rakats Fard, and then 2 rakats Sunnah

3. Work or Study

Resuming work, study, or other productive activities in accordance with Islamic values, such as honesty and diligence.

5. Charity and Good Deeds

Taking time to help others, whether through acts of kindness, assisting family, or donating to charity, as good deeds are encouraged throughout the day.

Evening Routine (After Maghrib)



1. Perform Maghrib Salah:

Pray the obligatory Maghrib prayer, along with sunnah prayer after Maghrib prayer.

---------------

2. Recite Evening Adhkar:

Spend time reciting the evening remembrances (adhkar).

Ayat al-Kursi (Surah Al-Baqarah: 255).

Surah Al-Ikhlas, Al-Falaq, and An-Nas (3 times each).

General dhikr, such as:

SubhanAllahi wa bihamdihi (100 times).

La ilaha illallahu wahdahu la sharika lahu... (recited 10 or 100 times).



---------------

3. Family Time:

Spend time connecting with family or engaging in meaningful conversations.

---------------

4. Recite Qur'an:

Dedicate some time to recite or reflect upon the Qur’an.

---------------

Night Routine (Before Bed)

1. Perform Isha Salah:

Pray the obligatory Isha prayer along with any sunnah or witr prayers.

---------------

2. Prepare for Bed (Sunnah Practices):

Perform wudu before sleeping.

Dust and clean your bed with your hands three times, as per the sunnah.

---------------

3. Recite Bedtime Duas and Adhkar:

Surah Al-Mulk (recommended before sleep for protection from the punishment of the grave).

Ayat al-Kursi (for protection).

Last two verses of Surah Al-Baqarah.

Say the following three times:

Bismika Allahumma amutu wa ahya ("In Your name, O Allah, I live and die.").

---------------

4. Reflect and Make Dua:

Reflect on your deeds for the day, seeking forgiveness for any shortcomings. Make dua for yourself, your family, the ummah, and any personal needs. ---------------

5. Sleep on Your Right Side:

Lie on your right side, facing the Qiblah if possible, as the Prophet (ﷺ) recommended.

---------------

Optional Acts (For Night Worship)

1. Wake Up for Tahajjud:

If possible, wake up in the last third of the night to pray Tahajjud. It’s a time when duas are most accepted.

---------------

2. Recite More Qur’an or Engage in Dhikr:

Spend this quiet time in remembrance of Allah, seeking His mercy and forgiveness.

This routine helps maintain a balance between worship, family, and personal reflection while aligning with the practices of the Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ).
